Market Analysis and Performance Indicators

Introduction

This guide provides detailed explanations of the key metrics and insights used in our market analysis dashboards. It is designed to help decision-makers understand the technical aspects of our market performance indicators and their business implications.

Each metric is explained in terms of:

  • What it measures
  • How it's calculated
  • How to interpret different values
  • Business implications and actionable insights
  • Practical examples where applicable

The metrics are organized into categories that align with our dashboard structure, making it easy to reference when reviewing market performance.

Brand Health Metrics

Brand health metrics measure consumer perceptions and attitudes toward our destination. These metrics are leading indicators that typically precede changes in actual travel behavior.

Awareness

27.38% (+2.38pp)

Awareness measures the percentage of the target audience who are aware of Abu Dhabi as a Cultural and Sea & Sun travel destination.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which international holiday destinations offering a combination of both cultural and sun & beach activities are you aware of?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of the total surveyed population.

Interpretation:
  • 90%+: Excellent awareness, approaching market saturation
  • 70-90%: Strong awareness, but room for improvement
  • 50-70%: Moderate awareness, significant opportunity for growth
  • <50%: Low awareness, major opportunity but requires substantial investment
Business Implications:
  • At 27.38%, South Korea has low awareness of Abu Dhabi, indicating a significant opportunity for growth but requiring substantial investment.
  • The +2.38pp year-over-year growth suggests positive momentum in awareness building efforts.
  • This low awareness level represents both a challenge and an opportunity - while requiring significant investment to build awareness, it also means there is substantial untapped potential in this market.
  • Marketing should prioritize broad awareness-building campaigns before focusing on conversion to consideration and intent.
  • The positive trend in awareness provides a foundation for continued marketing efforts, but the low base suggests a need for sustained investment over time.
  • Awareness-building efforts should focus on Abu Dhabi's unique selling propositions that would most appeal to South Korean travelers, particularly cultural attractions and luxury experiences.

Consideration

18.81% (+4.81pp)

Consideration measures the percentage of aware consumers who would consider travelling to Abu Dhabi in the next 3 years.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations would you like to visit in the next 3 years?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are aware of Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 70%+: Excellent consideration, strong position in consumer mindset
  • 50-70%: Good consideration, competitive position
  • 30-50%: Moderate consideration, significant barriers exist
  • <30%: Low consideration, major perception issues to address
Business Implications:
  • At 18.81%, consideration is in the low range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from awareness to consideration.
  • However, the +4.81pp increase shows a strong positive trend that suggests marketing efforts are effectively addressing some barriers.
  • This significant increase in consideration (+4.81pp) despite low awareness suggests that those who do become aware of Abu Dhabi are increasingly likely to consider it as a destination.
  • The gap between awareness (27.38%) and consideration (18.81%) is relatively small compared to other markets, suggesting that the main challenge is building initial awareness rather than converting awareness to consideration.
  • Marketing should continue to focus on both awareness building and addressing specific barriers to consideration, with particular emphasis on knowledge gaps about Abu Dhabi's offerings.
  • The strong growth in consideration suggests that current messaging is resonating with South Korean travelers who become aware of Abu Dhabi.

Intent

6.39% (+0.39pp)

Intent measures the percentage of considering consumers who intend to visit Abu Dhabi in the next 12 months.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations are you likely to visit in the next 12 months?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are considering Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 50%+: Excellent intent, very strong conversion potential
  • 30-50%: Good intent, healthy conversion potential
  • 20-30%: Moderate intent, conversion challenges exist
  • <20%: Low intent, significant conversion barriers
Business Implications:
  • At 6.39%, intent is in the very low range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from consideration to intent.
  • The modest +0.39pp increase shows minimal improvement, suggesting persistent barriers to converting consideration to actual travel plans.
  • This suggests that while South Korean travelers are increasingly considering Abu Dhabi (strong consideration growth), they face significant barriers to making concrete travel plans.
  • The large gap between consideration (18.81%) and intent (6.39%) indicates specific barriers that prevent those who consider Abu Dhabi from planning to visit in the near term.
  • Marketing should focus on addressing practical barriers to booking (visa process, flight availability, etc.) and creating urgency through limited-time offers or highlighting seasonal experiences.
  • The low intent level suggests a need for tactical, conversion-focused campaigns that address specific barriers identified in market research.
  • Partnerships with South Korean travel agencies and airlines could help address some of the practical barriers to booking.

Intent/Consideration Ratio

0.34 (-0.06)

This ratio measures the efficiency of converting consideration to intent, indicating how effectively we move consumers down the funnel.

How It's Calculated:

Intent percentage divided by Consideration percentage. For example: 6.39% ÷ 18.81% = 0.34

Interpretation:
  • 0.8+: Excellent conversion efficiency, minimal drop-off
  • 0.6-0.8: Good conversion efficiency
  • 0.4-0.6: Moderate conversion efficiency, significant drop-off
  • <0.4: Poor conversion efficiency, major funnel leakage
Business Implications:
  • At 0.34, the conversion from consideration to intent is poor, indicating that only 34% of those who consider Abu Dhabi intend to visit in the next 12 months.
  • The decrease of -0.06 suggests a worsening conversion efficiency, which is concerning.
  • This indicates that while consideration is growing strongly (+4.81pp), intent is not keeping pace (+0.39pp), resulting in a widening gap between these metrics.
  • The declining ratio suggests that new barriers may be emerging or existing barriers are becoming more significant for South Korean travelers.
  • Marketing should focus on understanding and addressing the specific barriers that are preventing consideration from converting to intent, particularly the knowledge gap (40% of non-visitors cite lack of knowledge) and safety concerns (22%, +6pp).
  • The declining ratio (-0.06) is a warning sign that requires immediate attention to prevent further deterioration of conversion efficiency.
  • Targeted messaging addressing specific barriers identified in the barriers to visitation research should be prioritized.

Market Characteristics

Market characteristics describe fundamental travel behaviors and patterns specific to the South Korea market. These metrics help inform tactical marketing decisions and campaign timing.

Average Length of Stay

2.9 days

The average number of nights South Korean travelers spend in Abu Dhabi per visit.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel guest data and visitor surveys (DVS), calculated as the sum of all nights stayed divided by the number of visitors.

Business Implications:
  • The 2.9-day average stay indicates a very short vacation timeframe, significantly shorter than many other markets.
  • This suggests marketing should focus on compact, high-impact itineraries and experiences that can be enjoyed within a 3-day timeframe.
  • Package offerings should be designed around this short timeframe to maximize appeal, focusing on experiences that can be enjoyed efficiently.
  • There's an opportunity to develop weekend or long-weekend packages that include Abu Dhabi as a short break destination.
  • Hotel partners should be encouraged to develop 3-day packages with all-inclusive options to appeal to this market.
  • The very short stay suggests a need for high-impact experiences that deliver maximum value in a limited time, focusing on the most iconic attractions and experiences.
  • There may also be an opportunity to extend the average stay by promoting multi-destination packages that combine Abu Dhabi with other nearby destinations.

Booking Window

97 days

The average time between booking and arrival for South Korean travelers visiting Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on booking data from hotels, flight booking engines and OTAs, calculated as the average number of days between booking confirmation and arrival date.

Business Implications:
  • The 97-day booking window is exceptionally long, indicating a market that plans trips well in advance.
  • Marketing campaigns should be timed to run approximately 3-4 months before target travel periods.
  • This extended window allows for considered decision-making and comparison shopping by travelers.
  • Early booking incentives are likely to be very effective for this market.
  • The long booking window suggests that both digital and traditional media can be effective, as there is ample time for consumers to respond to marketing messages.
  • This extended planning period also provides opportunities for upselling and package enhancements over time.
  • The 97-day window aligns with the 3.2-month lagged effect between media impressions and travel queries (R² of 0.67), suggesting that marketing efforts should account for this extended lead time.
Strategic Example:

For the October-December peak season, major campaign pushes should begin in July to align with the 97-day booking window. Early booking discounts offered 110+ days in advance could be particularly effective for this market, encouraging even earlier commitments. The media allocation model should account for the 3.2-month lag effect, with heavier investment in June-July to impact the October-December peak.

Peak Months (Hotel Guests)

Dec & Oct

The months with the highest volume of South Korean hotel guests in Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel occupancy data, identifying the months with the highest number of South Korean guests.

Business Implications:
  • December (3,842 guests) and October (3,215 guests) represent the clear peak season for South Korean travelers.
  • This bi-modal peak aligns with favorable weather conditions and South Korean holiday periods.
  • Marketing should ensure sufficient inventory and capacity during these months.
  • Pricing strategies can be optimized for these high-demand periods.
  • There's an opportunity to develop shoulder season campaigns (November, January) to extend the peak periods.
  • Summer months represent a significant growth opportunity through targeted campaigns highlighting indoor activities and special summer offers.
  • The October peak coincides with the Korean Thanksgiving holiday (Chuseok) when many South Koreans travel internationally, presenting a strategic opportunity for targeted marketing.

Advanced Statistical Metrics

These advanced metrics provide deeper insights into market dynamics, helping to quantify relationships between marketing activities and outcomes, forecast accuracy, and market behavior patterns.

Seasonality Index

2.3 (extreme seasonal variation)

A statistical measure that quantifies the degree of seasonal variation in the South Korea travel market data.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated by dividing the highest monthly value by the average monthly value in a year. For example, if December has a travel query index of 42.78 (the highest month) and the average monthly index is around 18.6, the seasonality index would be approximately 2.3 (42.78 ÷ 18.6).

Interpretation:
  • 1.0-1.3: Low seasonal variation (relatively flat demand throughout the year)
  • 1.3-1.6: Moderate seasonal variation (noticeable peaks and troughs)
  • 1.6-2.0: High seasonal variation (significant peaks and troughs)
  • 2.0+: Extreme seasonal variation (very pronounced peaks and low seasons)
Business Implications:
  • The 2.3 value indicates that the market has extreme seasonal fluctuations, with peak months having about 130% higher travel query activity than the average month.
  • This is significant enough to warrant very strong seasonal marketing strategies.
  • Marketing efforts should be heavily concentrated before peak periods (considering the 97-day booking window).
  • There's a substantial opportunity to grow travel interest during off-peak months through targeted campaigns.
  • Resource allocation should follow this seasonal pattern, with increased investment before peak periods.
  • The extreme seasonality suggests a need for aggressive differential pricing and promotion strategies across the year.
  • Special attention should be paid to the December and October peaks, with marketing campaigns beginning in September and July respectively, accounting for the 97-day booking window.

Media Elasticity

0.42 (moderate impact)

A marketing metric that measures how responsive travel queries are to changes in media impressions for the South Korea market.

How It's Calculated:

Calculated using regression analysis on historical data, comparing the percentage change in travel queries to the percentage change in media impressions over time.

Formula: Media Elasticity = % Change in Travel Queries ÷ % Change in Media Impressions

Interpretation:
  • <0.3: Low impact (minimal response to media investment)
  • 0.3-0.6: Moderate impact (reasonable response to media investment)
  • 0.6-1.0: High impact (strong response to media investment)
  • >1.0: Very high impact (exceptional response to media investment)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.42 elasticity means that for every 10% increase in media impressions, travel queries increase by approximately 4.2%.
  • This indicates that media investments drive travel queries with moderate efficiency.
  • The relationship is positive and moderately strong (well above 0.3).
  • Media campaigns will generate positive ROI with reasonable efficiency.
  • Doubling media spend will not double travel queries, but will produce meaningful growth.
  • The moderate elasticity suggests that the South Korea market is reasonably responsive to media campaigns, but other factors also influence travel decisions.
  • This moderate responsiveness suggests that media investments should be complemented by other marketing approaches, such as addressing specific barriers to visitation, particularly the knowledge gap (40%).
Strategic Example:

If you're planning to increase media impressions by 20% for a campaign targeting the South Korean market, you can expect approximately an 8.4% increase in travel queries (20% × 0.42 = 8.4%). This helps set realistic expectations for campaign performance and ROI calculations. Given the 97-day booking window, these increased queries would translate to actual visits approximately 3 months later.

YoY Growth Consistency

0.72 (high consistency)

A statistical measure that evaluates how uniform or predictable the year-over-year growth patterns are for the South Korea travel market.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated using a statistical measure like the coefficient of variation (inverse) or correlation coefficient of YoY growth rates across different time periods. The scale ranges from 0 to 1, where 0 represents completely inconsistent growth (highly volatile) and 1 represents perfectly consistent growth (same growth rate across all periods).

Interpretation:
  • <0.5: Low consistency (high volatility, unpredictable patterns)
  • 0.5-0.7: Moderate consistency (some predictability with variations)
  • 0.7-0.9: High consistency (reliable patterns with minor variations)
  • >0.9: Very high consistency (highly predictable patterns)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.72 consistency indicates that there is high predictability in how the market behaves year-over-year.
  • Forecasting can be done with good confidence.
  • Most months will show similar growth patterns to previous years.
  • Some months may show unexpected variations requiring adaptive strategies.
  • Marketing teams can plan with reasonable certainty about expected outcomes.
  • Historical patterns provide reliable guidance for future planning.
  • This high consistency (0.72) contributes to a relatively narrow forecast confidence interval of ±8.4%.

Forecast Confidence Interval

±8.4%

A statistical measure indicating the range within which we expect the actual 2025 travel query values to fall with a certain level of confidence (typically 95%).

How It's Calculated:

Derived from statistical analysis of historical forecast accuracy, market volatility, and model parameters. It represents the standard error of the forecast multiplied by a factor corresponding to the desired confidence level (typically 1.96 for 95% confidence).

Interpretation:
  • <5%: Very high forecast precision, highly reliable predictions
  • 5-10%: Good forecast precision, reliable predictions with moderate variance
  • 10-15%: Moderate forecast precision, usable predictions with significant variance
  • >15%: Low forecast precision, high uncertainty in predictions
Business Implications:
  • The ±8.4% confidence interval means that if we forecast a travel query index of 150.0 for a particular month, the actual value is expected to fall between 137.4 and 162.6 with 95% confidence.
  • This relatively narrow interval indicates a reliable forecast for the South Korea market.
  • Marketing teams should plan with appropriate contingencies, knowing the potential range of outcomes.
  • Budget allocations should include flexibility to adjust based on actual performance within this range.
  • The confidence interval suggests that forecasts can be used with good confidence for planning purposes, though with slightly less precision than some other markets.
  • The wider confidence interval compared to some markets (e.g., France at ±6.8%) suggests slightly higher volatility in the South Korean market's response patterns.

Barriers to Visitation

Understanding why potential visitors choose not to travel to Abu Dhabi is crucial for developing targeted marketing strategies that address specific concerns and misconceptions.

Top Barriers to Visit

5 key factors

The primary reasons South Korean travelers give for not considering Abu Dhabi as a travel destination, based on survey research.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "What are the main reasons you would not consider traveling to Abu Dhabi?" The percentages represent the proportion of respondents who selected each reason.

Current Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q4 2023):
  • Knowledge Gap: 40% (+2pp) don't know enough about Abu Dhabi
  • Previous Visitation: 35% (+4pp) have already visited Abu Dhabi before
  • Unfriendly Perception: 25% (+2pp) heard people are not friendly
  • Children's Entertainment: 22% (+7pp) feel there's not enough for children
  • Safety Concerns: 22% (+6pp) perceive it as unsafe
Business Implications:
  • Knowledge Gap (40%, +2pp): This remains the most significant barrier, indicating a need for increased educational content about Abu Dhabi's offerings. Marketing should focus on raising awareness about the destination's unique experiences and attractions.
  • Previous Visitation (35%, +4pp): The increase suggests that many South Korean travelers who have visited Abu Dhabi are not motivated to return. This indicates a need to develop and promote new attractions and experiences to encourage repeat visits.
  • Unfriendly Perception (25%, +2pp): This perception issue requires addressing through testimonials and content that showcases the welcoming nature of Abu Dhabi residents and hospitality staff.
  • Children's Entertainment (22%, +7pp): This significant increase suggests a growing perception that Abu Dhabi is not family-friendly, despite the presence of numerous family attractions. This misperception needs to be corrected through targeted family-focused marketing.
  • Safety Concerns (22%, +6pp): The substantial increase indicates a deteriorating perception of safety that must be addressed through communications highlighting Abu Dhabi's strong safety record and low crime rates.
Strategic Recommendation:

Develop a comprehensive "Discover Abu Dhabi" campaign specifically for the South Korean market that addresses the knowledge gap (40%) through educational content about the destination's offerings. Include family-focused content highlighting theme parks and child-friendly activities to address the perception of limited children's entertainment (22%, +7pp). Incorporate authentic testimonials from South Korean visitors to address both the unfriendly perception (25%) and safety concerns (22%).

Conclusion

This guide provides a comprehensive explanation of the key metrics used in our market analysis for South Korea. By understanding these metrics and their business implications, DCT - Abu Dhabi can make more informed strategic marketing decisions.

The South Korea market shows several distinctive characteristics:

  • Low awareness (27.38%) but growing (+2.38pp), indicating a market with significant untapped potential
  • Low consideration (18.81%) but strong growth (+4.81pp), suggesting effective messaging is resonating with those who become aware
  • Very low intent (6.39%) with minimal growth (+0.39pp), indicating significant barriers to conversion
  • Poor intent-to-consideration ratio (0.34) that is declining (-0.06), suggesting worsening conversion efficiency
  • Very short average stay (2.9 days) indicating compact vacation patterns
  • Exceptionally long booking window (97 days) allowing for extended campaign planning
  • Extreme seasonality with peaks in December and October
  • Moderate media elasticity (0.42) suggesting reasonable response to media investments
  • Strong interest in cultural attractions and shopping, with Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque and Louvre Abu Dhabi being the most searched attractions
  • Preference for luxury accommodations, with desert resorts and high-end international brands dominating searches
  • Significant barriers including knowledge gap (40%), previous visitation (35%), and unfriendly perception (25%)
  • Concerning growth in children's entertainment concerns (+7pp) and safety concerns (+6pp)

These insights suggest a strategic focus on:

  • Addressing the significant knowledge gap through educational content about Abu Dhabi's offerings
  • Highlighting family-friendly attractions and activities to counter the growing perception of limited children's entertainment
  • Emphasizing Abu Dhabi's safety record and security measures to address growing safety concerns
  • Showcasing new attractions and experiences to encourage repeat visits from previous visitors
  • Developing compact itineraries that maximize the very short 2.9-day average stay, focusing on high-impact cultural experiences and premium shopping
  • Timing campaigns to align with the exceptionally long 97-day booking window, particularly for the October-December peak period
  • Creating marketing content that showcases the blend of cultural attractions, luxury experiences, and shopping opportunities available in Abu Dhabi
  • Developing partnerships with luxury hotels and desert resorts that appeal to South Korean travelers' preferences
  • Positioning Abu Dhabi as a luxury cultural destination with unique architectural attractions and premium shopping
  • Emphasizing the diversity of experiences available to maximize engagement during the short 2.9-day average stay duration

With these insights and ongoing monitoring of these metrics, DCT - Abu Dhabi can formulate more effective marketing strategies and increase our return on marketing investment in the South Korea market. The significant increase in planned media impressions for 2025 (371.3M, +151.06% YoY) should drive substantial growth in travel queries, particularly for the October-December peak period, leveraging the market's moderate media elasticity (0.42).
